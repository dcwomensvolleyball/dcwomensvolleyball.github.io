    • The premier ladies' volleyball league in the District of Columbia!

    About DC Women's Volleyball: Founded in the fall of 2016, we are independently run with the goal of growing a competitive community for women’s volleyball in DC. Our average league member has experience playing competitive volleyball throughout high school and college (e.g., club, DIII, DII, DI).

    Our league has the unique feature of having a captains' draft each season. This allows players more opportunity to play with different people and to continue/enhance their skill development (e.g., by trying different positions they may not have played in college), thus increasing competition.

    League Founder & Commissioner: Katie Solook

    Level of Play: This league is targeted for 'A' level players and above. Elite high school, club, or college level player that can run different offenses (e.g., 6-2, 5-1, etc.), have a great understanding of all positions on the court, and can execute all skills with accuracy and power. Few player errors and fast game play.

    League Structure/Information: Women's 6s indoor. Participants sign up as individuals and teams are selected via captains' draft. Matches are on Tuesday evenings at 7 or 8pm.

    Location: National Cathedral School, Underwood Athletic Center (see directions here)

    Questions or interested in joining the league? Email dcwomensvolleyball@gmail.com with your name, volleyball background, preferred position(s), availability, and best contact method.

    LINK TO CURRENT SEASON

    Three games - First two sets are to 21, third set is to 15. There is a cap of 25 for the first two sets. Cap is 17 for the third set. If we're running out of time, to "win" the third set, you must have at least 7 points. If fewer points are scored, the third set is considered a tie.
    Call your own - We don't have refs, but highly encourage a 'call your own' doubles, foot faults, back row attack, and net violations mentality. If we all are vigilant about calling things on our own team, then we don't need to call them across the net. If there is a call that is up for debate, please feel free to call a replay.
    If a team has MORE than 3 non-roster subs, they would start down by 5 points (or rather, the opposing team would be given 5 additional points to start). For playoffs, substitutions must be approved by opposing team captain. Overall, regardless of playoffs, please try to substitute like for like in terms of skill level.
    The gym is great and sets up the nets for us... but unfortunately they don't always set it to the correct height. If you get there early, please please please help with adjusting it. (see instructions here)
    The earliest they will let us into the gym is 6:50pm. I encourage everyone to take advantage of that if you have the early game for warm-up time to ensure we start on time and get out on time.
    Please be mindful of gathering all your belongings (including balls) and leaving the gym promptly - ideally no later than 9:05pm.
