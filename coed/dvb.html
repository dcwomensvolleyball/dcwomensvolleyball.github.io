    • As a self-regulated league, we expect teams to be honest and judicious in calling their own violations. Examples include:
    • In/out line calls and antennae contacts
    • Net touches
    • Double contacts
    • Block touches
    When six (6) or more players are present, There must be three (3) women on the court at all times. One can be a libero and allowed to serve in one rotation. Three (3) women on the court rule is suspended only when the opposite of the libero is serving. No girl touch rule applies.
    • If your team is short of three (3) women, it's okay to start with fewer women as long as you have no more than three (3) guys on the court. So three (3) guys and two (2) women or three (3) guys and one (1) woman; no ghost rule applies.
    On each court, match #1 has a hard stop at 8:00pm and match #2 has a hard stop at 9:00pm.
    When possible, we will give you a warning that your match needs to wrap up/call “last point” at 7:59pm and 8:59pm. This is out of courtesy to you, but ultimately it is on each team to be aware of the time and end promptly at 8pm and 9pm.
    There is a hard start time of 7:10pm and 8:10pm.
    • If at start time (7:10 or 8:10) all of your players have not arrived, you can either find a sub that just happens to be at the gym or start short.
    • If you deliberately choose to start late despite someone calling for the match to begin, you risk forfeiting points for any games not played.
    Teams will play three sets. The first two sets are to 25 points, with a cap of 27 points. The third set is to 15 points, with a cap of 17 points.
    • The first two sets will be worth 1.0 set point, while the third set will be worth 0.5 set points.
    • If you hit time and the third set is tied or has yet to begin, each team gets 0.25 points for the third set.
    Generally, subs should be of equivalent skill level to the player(s) being replaced.
    During playoffs, subs must be run past the opposing team's captain prior to the match.
    • If there are disagreements on if a sub is of equivalent skill level, the dispute should be brought to a steering committee member.
    The pursuit rule is permitted. Depending on the object interfering, there are different rules for if continuation is allowed.
    Basketball hoops and their extensions (back and side hoops) - includes the connector (the black beams), backboard, etc.:
    • When the hoops are up, they are fair game and considered in-bounds to play off of.
    • When the hoops are down, the ball is considered dead upon contact unless it interfered with the play and a player was there/could have realistically gotten to the ball had the hoop not interfered.
    Walls, court dividers (the cloth "walls"), and any part of the running track:
    • Ball is considered dead upon contact.
    Ceiling fixtures (beams, lamps, etc.) above the court:
    • As long as the ball remains on the same side of the net, a ball hitting the ceiling fixtures is playable.
    • If the ball hits a ceiling fixture and immediately travels over the net, it is a dead ball and the other team's point.
    Ropes:
    • When the ropes are hanging over the court and a ball hits them during play, it is considered an automatic replay.
    Playoff seeding tiebreakers are as follows:
    1. Set Points
    2. Head-to-Head
    3. Greater # of 2.5 Set Point Wins
    4. Coin Flip
    In the event teams split the first two sets and time is called before the 3rd set begins, the team with the most combined points scored advances.
